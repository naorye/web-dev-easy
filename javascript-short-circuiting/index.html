The idea behind this lays in the way the JavaScript enging works. For the && operator, expressions are executed until the first “false” value. For the || operator, expressions are executed until the first “true” value. Another thing that helps us here is the fact that “null” and “undefined” are treated as “false” and other than that are treated as “true” (except zero).
